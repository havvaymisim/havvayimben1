💧 Water Pollution

Textile dyeing is the second-largest polluter of water globally. The fashion industry uses 93 billion cubic meters of water annually.

🏭 Chemical Impact

Over 3,500 chemicals are used in textile production, many of which are harmful to human health and the environment.

🗑️ Textile Waste

The equivalent of one garbage truck full of clothes is burned or dumped in landfills every second worldwide.

🌍 Microplastics

Synthetic clothing releases 500,000 tons of microfibers into the ocean each year - equivalent to 50 billion plastic bottles.

Sustainable Fashion Tips

Buy Less, Choose Well

Invest in quality pieces that last longer rather than fast fashion items.

Swap & Share

Organize clothing swaps with friends or use clothing rental services for special occasions.

Repair & Upcycle

Learn basic sewing skills to extend your clothes' life and transform old pieces.

Choose Eco-Fabrics

Look for organic cotton, linen, hemp, and recycled materials when shopping.

Donate Responsibly

Give clothes a second life by donating to local charities or textile recycling programs.

Care Properly

Wash clothes in cold water, air dry when possible, and follow care instructions to extend lifespan.
